Securing Information

 

At the end of the lesson, you should be able to:

  • Identify payment models used in e-commerce, including payment gateways.
  • Identify issues related to working in a global environment, including different currencies, multi-lingual issues, international shipping, legal and regulatory issues.
  • Identify the importance of SSL/TLS to a transaction that contains sensitive information.

 

Is your personal information secure?  We all trust that the individuals that we do business with on the Web are taking every precaution to safeguard your personal information.  As a developer working on a e-commerce site, there is a great responsibility to do everything that you can protect information.

Common sense rules for a Web developer:

  • Don't ask for any information that is not needed
  • Don't store any information that is not needed either in a database or with JavaScript cookies
    • If you are using cookies to remember information, be sure you delete any sensitive information from your end users computer.
  • Have clear and concise information on how the information that is collected will be used

SSL / TSL

Collection of sensitive information should always be done across secure channels.  On the Web, that means either through secure socket layer (SSL) or Transport Layer Security (TLS).
