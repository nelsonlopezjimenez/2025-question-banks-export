At the end of the lesson, you should be able to:

  • Identify the uses and benefits of various document and multimedia file formats, including PDF, RTF, PostScript, EPS, MOV, MPEG, streaming media, nonstreaming media.
  • Estimate download time for Web pages.
  • Document results of Web site functionality testing.
  • Identify the importance of online indexing and cataloging.

 

3.5 seconds... That is the estimated time that an end-user will look at your page if something doesn't attract them to stay.  Not much time.  Successful keys are:

  • Branding - make sure your end-user knows where they are
  • Limiting download time effectively - limit the use of over-sized graphics and multimedia files.  This is often referred to as "page weight". 

Many HTML editors have built-in capabilities to test the download speed of a Web page.  You can check a page download time at http://tools.pingdom.com/fpt/. If you think your pages are getting to heavy, consider breaking the pages up into smaller bites and chunks.
